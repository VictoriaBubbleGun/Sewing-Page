Obsession with Sewing

A Hobby with a long History

The art of sewing is at least 20,000 years old. Ancient peoples joined pieces of material using bone and horn needles and animal sinew for thread. Around the 14th century iron needles were invented, and by the 15th century there were eyed needles. Later sewing needles were made of steel, as they still are today. In the 1800s the first practical sewing machine appeared, and today most sewing is done by machine, though some sewers still choose to perform fine sewing and finishing by hand.
For me sewing holds a special place in my heart because I cherish the feeling of donning garments I've meticulously crafted myself. The knowledge that I'm wearing something truly unique, an expression of my personal style and creativity, brings a sense of pride that's unmatched. Moreover, the sustainability of sewing is a core reason for my affection. By repurposing fabrics, upcycling old clothes, and reducing waste, I'm contributing to a more eco-friendly way of dressing, aligning with my values and making a positive impact on the planet.
